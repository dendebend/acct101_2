Q1. INVENTORIABLE COSTS
a) A corporation uses the perpetual inventory and the gross method of accounting for its inventory.
   On March 1, it purchased $80,000 of inventory, terms 2/10, n/30.
   On March 3, it returned $8,000 of goods to the supplier.
   On March 9, it paid the supplier.
   What is the net cost of this purchase?

   ANSWER: $70,560
   CALCULATION: ($80,000 - $8,000) × 0.98 = $72,000 × 0.98 = $70,560

b) A corporation uses the perpetual inventory and the gross method of accounting for its inventory.
   On April 10, it purchased $50,000 of inventory, terms 3/15, n/30.
   On April 15, it returned $5,000 of goods to the supplier.
   On April 22, it paid the supplier.
   How much was the purchase discount taken?

   ANSWER: $1,350
   CALCULATION: ($50,000 - $5,000) × 0.03 = $45,000 × 0.03 = $1,350

Q9. PETTY CASH: CASH SHORT AND OVER
a) A petty cash fund is established for $300. On the 15th of the month, the petty cash drawer
   contains $180 in cash and $115 in receipts for expenditures/disbursements out of the fund.
   If there is a discrepancy, how would you describe the amount of the cash short and over?
   Be sure to indicate whether the amount is a shortage or an overage.

   ANSWER: $5 shortage
   CALCULATION: $180 + $115 = $295, but fund is $300, so $5 shortage

b) A petty cash fund is established for $500. On the last day of the month, the fund was replenished.
   The journal entry for replenishment of the petty cash fund included a credit to Cash for $463
   and a credit to Cash Short and Over for $2. How much was expended out of the petty cash fund in
   the month?

   ANSWER: $465
   CALCULATION: Total credits = $463 + $2 = $465 (this equals total debits/expenses)

Q11. LOWER OF COST OR MARKET VALUE (LCM)
The inventory of Oheto Company on December 31 consists of the following items:

Part         Quantity    Cost Per Unit    Market Value Per Unit
Wallets:
  110          600          $ 95                $ 100
  111        1,000          $ 60                $  52
Bill clips:
  112          500          $ 80                $  76
  113          200          $170                $ 180
Coin purses:
  120          400          $205                $ 208
  122          300          $240                $ 235

What is the inventory to be reported on the December 31 balance sheet?

ANSWER: Apply LCM to each item:
110: $95 × 600 = $57,000 (cost is lower)
111: $52 × 1,000 = $52,000 (market is lower)
112: $76 × 500 = $38,000 (market is lower)
113: $170 × 200 = $34,000 (cost is lower)
120: $205 × 400 = $82,000 (cost is lower)
122: $235 × 300 = $70,500 (market is lower)
Total: $57,000 + $52,000 + $38,000 + $34,000 + $82,000 + $70,500 = $333,500

Q12i. In periods of rising prices, what will LIFO produce?
a) The same net income as FIFO
b) Higher net income than FIFO
c) Higher net income than average costing
d) Lower net income than FIFO

ANSWER: d) Lower net income than FIFO
EXPLANATION: LIFO uses newest (expensive) inventory for COGS → higher COGS → lower net income
