1 Unit 06: Merchandising Operations (12 Questions)
1. A corporation uses the perpetual inventory and gross method. On March 1, it purchased $80,000 of inventory, terms 2/10, n/30. On March 3, it returned $8,000 of goods. On March 9, it paid the supplier. What is the net cost?
($80,000 - $8,000) × 0.98 = $72,000 × 0.98
2. A corporation uses perpetual inventory and gross method. On April 10, purchased $50,000, terms 3/15, n/30. On April 15, returned $5,000. On April 22, paid supplier. How much purchase discount was taken?
($50,000 - $5,000) × 0.03
3. Jan. 10: Liana Lions purchased $13,000 merchandise, terms 2/10, n/30, FOB shipping point from Gabrielle Gators. What is the buyer's (Liana Lions) purchase entry?
Perpetual + gross method = record at full invoice amount
4. Same transaction: What entries should seller (Gabrielle Gators) record on Jan. 10? (Cost was $9,200)
Perpetual system needs TWO entries: sale + cost
5. June 13 sale: $8,750, terms 4/10, n/30. June 16: $500 return. June 23: customer paid. How much cash received?
($8,750 - $500) × 0.96
6. Fred Fryers: Sales $10,200,000; Sales returns $277,000; Sales discounts $6,750. What is net sales?
Net Sales = Sales - Returns - Discounts
7. Fred Fryers: Purchases $4,730,000; Purch returns $14,745; Purchase discounts $2,400; Freight in $3,000. What is net purchases?
Net Purch = Purch - Returns - Disc + Freight
8. Fred Fryers: Inventory Jan 1 $2,700,000; Net purchases (from Q7) $4,715,855; Inventory Dec 31 $4,875,000. What is COGS?
COGS = Beg Inv + Net Purch - End Inv
9. Fred Fryers: Net sales (from Q6) $9,916,250; COGS (from Q8) $2,540,855. What is gross profit?
Gross Profit = Net Sales - COGS
10. Martin Co purchases $4,200 merchandise on March 1, terms 3/10, n/30. If paid on March 1, what is the cost?
3/10 means 3% discount if paid within 10 days

12. Net income $15,000, operating expenses $20,000, net sales $75,000. What is COGS?

3 Unit 08: Merchandise Inventory (7 Questions)
22. Constance Corp periodic inventory data: Beg 10,000@$6.00; Purch 12,000@$6.10; Purch 15,000@$6.25; Purch 3,000@$6.50; Purch 8,000@$6.60. Sales $540,000 @ $15/unit.
(10,000×$6) + (12,000×$6.10) + (15,000×$6.25) + (3,000×$6.50) + (8,000×$6.60)
23. Using same Constance Corp data (COGA $303,450, units sold 36,000, units available 48,000, ending inventory 12,000 units). What is FIFO ending inventory value?
FIFO: 8,000@$6.60 + 3,000@$6.50 + 1,000@$6.25
24. Kam Co: Inv Jan 1: 8,000@$11; Purch June 19: 13,000@$12; Purch Nov 8: 5,000@$13. If 9,000 units on hand Dec 31, what is FIFO ending inventory (periodic)?
FIFO: newest purchases. 5,000@$13 + 4,000@$12
25. Same Kam Co data. What is LIFO ending inventory (periodic)?
LIFO: oldest purchases. 8,000@$11 + 1,000@$12
26. In periods of rising prices, what will LIFO produce?
LIFO sells newest (expensive) inventory → higher COGS → ?
27. Hagger Sounds LCM: iPods cost $24,000 market $20,400; Cell phones cost $18,000 market $19,000; DVDs cost $28,000 market $25,600. Ending inventory value (LCM by category)?
Take lower for each: iPods $20,400 + Phones $18,000 + DVDs $25,600
