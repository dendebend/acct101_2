Reconciling Book Balance and Bank Balance

What is Bank Reconciliation?

A bank reconciliation explains the difference between the cash balance in the company's books and the cash balance on the bank statement. Differences arise due to timing and errors.

Book Balance Adjustments

Items the company doesn't know about yet:

+ Collections by bank (EFT deposits)
Company doesn't know bank collected on their behalf
+ Interest earned
Bank adds interest, company learns from statement
- NSF checks
Deposited checks that bounced
- Bank service charges
Monthly fees company learns from statement
± Book errors
Errors in company's records
Requires Journal Entries!
Book adjustments need journal entries to correct company records
Bank Balance Adjustments

Items the bank doesn't know about yet:

+ Deposits in transit
Deposited by company but not yet recorded by bank
- Outstanding checks
Checks written but not yet cashed/cleared
± Bank errors
Errors in bank's records (rare)
No Journal Entries!
Bank adjustments are just for reconciliation - no entries needed
