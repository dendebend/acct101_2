Master Credit Terms like 2/10, n/30

Understanding Credit Terms
2/10, n/30
Discount %
2%
Discount offered if paid early
Discount Period
10 days
Must pay within this time to get discount
Credit Period
30 days
Net (full) amount due by this date
Read as:
"2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise full amount (net) is due in 30 days"
Common Credit Terms:
  • 2/10, n/30 - 2% discount if paid in 10 days, net due in 30 days
  • 3/15, n/45 - 3% discount if paid in 15 days, net due in 45 days
  • 1/10, n/60 - 1% discount if paid in 10 days, net due in 60 days
  • n/30 - No discount, net due in 30 days
  • EOM (End of Month) - Payment due at end of month

Practice Scenarios

1. On March 5, you purchase $5,000 of inventory on account with terms 2/10, n/30. You pay on March 12. How much do you pay?
Days elapsed: 7 days (within discount period)
Discount amount: $5,000 × 2% = $100
Amount paid: $5,000 - $100 = $4,900
Journal Entry (Perpetual):
Dr. Accounts Payable   5,000
    Cr. Inventory             100
    Cr. Cash                4,900
2. You sell $8,000 of merchandise on April 10 with terms 3/15, n/45. Customer pays on April 20. What is your sales discount?
Days elapsed: 10 days (within discount period)
Discount amount: $8,000 × 3% = $240
Cash received: $8,000 - $240 = $7,760
Journal Entry:
Dr. Cash                  7,760
Dr. Sales Discounts       240
    Cr. Accounts Receivable     8,000
3. Invoice for $12,000 dated May 1 with terms 2/10, n/30. You pay on May 15. Do you get the discount?
Days elapsed: 14 days (PAST discount period of 10 days)
Discount amount: $0 (no discount)
Amount paid: $12,000 (full amount)
Journal Entry:
Dr. Accounts Payable   12,000
    Cr. Cash                12,000
No discount - paid after discount period!
4. Why would a company offer 2/10, n/30 terms? What's the advantage?
For the seller:
  • Gets cash faster (better cash flow)
  • Reduces collection risk
  • Reduces accounts receivable balance
  • Less money tied up in receivables
For the buyer:
  • Saves money (2% discount)
  • 2% savings in 20 days ≈ 36% annual rate!
  • Almost always worth taking if you have cash
