Multi-Step vs Single-Step Income Statement

Multi-Step: Separates operating activities from non-operating activities. Shows gross profit, operating income, and net income separately.

Single-Step: Simply lists all revenues and all expenses with one subtraction to get net income.

Sales Revenue
- Cost of Goods Sold
= GROSS PROFIT
- Operating Expenses
= OPERATING INCOME
± Other Revenues and Expenses
= NET INCOME

Format Guide:
1. Net Sales Section
Sales Revenue - Sales Returns - Sales Discounts
2. Gross Profit Section
Net Sales - Cost of Goods Sold
3. Operating Income Section
Gross Profit - Operating Expenses (Selling + Admin)
4. Net Income Section
Operating Income ± Other Revenues/Expenses
Key Concepts:
• Gross Profit: What you make from selling merchandise before expenses
• Operating Income: Profit from core business operations
• Operating Expenses: Include selling expenses (sales salaries, advertising) and administrative expenses (office salaries, utilities)
• Other Revenues/Expenses: Non-operating items like interest, gains/losses on asset sales
