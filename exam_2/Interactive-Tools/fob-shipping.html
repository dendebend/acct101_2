FOB Shipping Terms

Free On Board: Who Pays Freight and Who Owns Goods in Transit?

What does FOB mean?
FOB = Free On Board - Specifies the point at which ownership transfers from seller to buyer and who pays for freight costs.
FOB Shipping Point

Ownership transfers at the seller's location (shipping point)

  • Buyer pays freight costs
  • Buyer owns goods during transit
  • Risk transfers immediately when shipped
  • Buyer records "Freight-In" (part of inventory cost)
  • Goods in transit belong to buyer
Buyer's Entry (Perpetual):
Dr. Inventory (cost + freight)
    Cr. Cash/A/P
FOB Destination

Ownership transfers at the buyer's location (destination)

  • Seller pays freight costs
  • Seller owns goods during transit
  • Risk stays with seller until delivery
  • Seller records "Freight-Out" (operating expense)
  • Goods in transit belong to seller
Seller's Entry (Freight-Out):
Dr. Freight-Out Expense
    Cr. Cash
