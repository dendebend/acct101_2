Lower of Cost or Market (LCM)

Inventory Valuation Rule

What is LCM?
The LCM Rule:

Conservatism Principle: When inventory's market value falls below its cost, write it down to market value.

This ensures inventory isn't overstated on the balance sheet and recognizes losses when they occur (not when inventory is sold).

Inventory Value = LOWER of (Cost or Market)
When to Apply LCM:
  • Obsolescence: Product becomes outdated (old tech, fashion)
  • Damage: Physical deterioration
  • Price decline: Market prices drop
  • Overstock: Too much inventory, clearance needed
Important Notes:
• If Market > Cost: Use Cost (don't write UP)
• If Market < Cost: Use Market (write DOWN)
• Can apply to individual items, categories, or total inventory
• Under IFRS, called "Lower of Cost or Net Realizable Value (NRV)"
